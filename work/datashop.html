1. Project Overview

DataShop is managed by CMU's LearnLab, which is part of the Simon Initiative.

My Role

I conducted a UI/UX facelift of the DataShop @CMU website as my first project under PLUS. My design went live on June 2nd, 2024. Visit the new and improved DataShop here.

2. Final Product

[Media Placeholder: GIF of new datashop site]

We implicitly trust online services/apps that feel and look modern. In order to maintain and ensure DataShop's credibility, I completed a UI revamp of the site.

3. Problem Space

DataShop is meant to be an application to facilitate novel research in the learning science field. However, the site's UI was incredibly outdated, making DataShop feel like an abandoned tool of the past.

[Media Placeholder: Old Datashop screenshots]

The site looked outdated, but it was still functional and useful. My goal was to show researchers this is true by modernizing DataShop's UI.

4. Design Solution

Main Focus

Preserving familiarity for existing users, while improving visual credibility to maintain and increase users.

5. Process & Reasoning

Why Primer?

Choosing between the CMU and Primer Design System, I ultimately went with Primer for two reasons:
1. Primer's primary colors are white and blue, which maintain DataShop's old primary colors.
2. Primer's minimal, modern look is instantly recognizable and trustworthy.

6. Reflection

This was the first project I completed where I worked within the context of developers and researchers. I learned how to present my design work to an audience and clearly communicate why I made certain design decisions.

Impact

After handing off the designs to DataShop devs, in June the changes were public!
Cindy (the software lead) mentioned there were many positive comments from DataShop users regarding the updated designs.
